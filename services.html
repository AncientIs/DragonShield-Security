Penetration Testing Services

Comprehensive offensive security assessments designed to identify, exploit, and help remediate vulnerabilities across your entire attack surface.

Network Penetration Testing

Our network penetration testing services provide a thorough evaluation of your internal and external network infrastructure. Our certified testers simulate real-world attacks to identify exploitable vulnerabilities in your firewalls, routers, switches, servers, workstations, and network services.

We conduct both external assessments — targeting your internet-facing perimeter — and internal assessments that simulate an attacker who has gained initial access to your corporate network. Our testers map your network topology, enumerate services, identify misconfigurations, and attempt to escalate privileges and move laterally to reach critical assets.

What We Test

  • External perimeter devices and services (firewalls, VPNs, DMZ hosts)
  • Internal network segmentation and access controls
  • Active Directory and domain infrastructure
  • Network services (DNS, SMTP, SNMP, SMB, RDP, SSH)
  • Patch management and vulnerability exposure
  • Privilege escalation and lateral movement paths
  • Data exfiltration opportunities

Deliverables

  • Comprehensive vulnerability report with CVSS scoring
  • Network topology and attack path diagrams
  • Prioritized remediation recommendations
  • Executive summary for leadership stakeholders
  • Post-remediation verification testing (optional)

Web Application Penetration Testing

Our web application penetration testing goes far beyond automated scanning. Our experts perform manual, in-depth security assessments of your web applications following the OWASP Web Security Testing Guide (WSTG) methodology. We identify vulnerabilities that automated tools routinely miss — including complex business logic flaws, chained attack vectors, and context-specific security weaknesses.

Whether you are testing a customer-facing portal, an internal business application, a RESTful API, or a single-page application, our team has the expertise to uncover the risks that matter most.

What We Test

  • OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities (injection, broken authentication, XSS, CSRF, etc.)
  • Authentication and session management mechanisms
  • Authorization and access control enforcement
  • Input validation and output encoding
  • Business logic vulnerabilities
  • API security (REST, GraphQL, SOAP)
  • File upload and data handling
  • Cryptographic implementation
  • Server-side request forgery (SSRF)
  • Error handling and information disclosure

Deliverables

  • Detailed findings report aligned with OWASP categories
  • Proof-of-concept demonstrations for each vulnerability
  • Risk-rated findings with business impact context
  • Remediation guidance with code-level recommendations
  • Retest to validate fixes (included in engagement)

Mobile Application Penetration Testing

Mobile applications present unique security challenges that require specialized testing expertise. Our mobile application penetration testing covers both iOS and Android platforms, examining the application binary, its communication with backend services, local data storage, and integration with device features.

We assess your mobile application against the OWASP Mobile Application Security Verification Standard (MASVS) and the OWASP Mobile Security Testing Guide (MSTG), ensuring comprehensive coverage of mobile-specific attack vectors.

What We Test

  • Insecure data storage on the device
  • Transport layer security and certificate pinning
  • Authentication and authorization mechanisms
  • Client-side injection vulnerabilities
  • Reverse engineering and code tampering resilience
  • Binary protections and anti-debugging measures
  • Backend API security
  • Inter-process communication (IPC) vulnerabilities
  • Cryptographic implementation
  • Privacy and data leakage concerns

Deliverables

  • Platform-specific vulnerability report (iOS and/or Android)
  • Risk assessment aligned with OWASP MASVS
  • Proof-of-concept exploits with screenshots and recordings
  • Remediation recommendations for development teams
  • Guidance on secure mobile development practices

Social Engineering Assessments

Technology alone cannot protect your organization if your people are vulnerable. Our social engineering assessments evaluate your organization's human security layer by simulating the tactics, techniques, and procedures used by real-world attackers to manipulate employees into compromising security.

We design realistic, customized social engineering campaigns that test your staff's ability to recognize and respond to threats — from sophisticated phishing emails to pretexting phone calls and physical intrusion attempts. Every campaign is conducted ethically and with full management authorization.

What We Test

  • Email phishing campaigns (spear phishing, credential harvesting, payload delivery)
  • Vishing (voice phishing) and pretexting calls
  • SMS phishing (smishing) campaigns
  • Physical security and tailgating assessments
  • USB drop and baiting scenarios
  • Impersonation and pretext development
  • Employee security awareness and reporting behavior

Deliverables

  • Campaign results with detailed metrics (click rates, credential submissions, reporting rates)
  • Individual and departmental susceptibility analysis
  • Anonymized findings to protect employee privacy
  • Recommendations for security awareness training improvements
  • Benchmark data for measuring progress over time

Red Team Operations

Red team engagements are our most comprehensive offensive security offering. Unlike traditional penetration testing, which focuses on identifying vulnerabilities within a defined scope, red team operations simulate a full-scale, realistic attack against your organization with minimal constraints.

Our red team operators employ the same tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) used by advanced persistent threats (APTs) and sophisticated adversaries. We target your people, processes, and technology simultaneously — combining technical exploitation, social engineering, and physical security testing to achieve defined objectives such as accessing sensitive data, compromising critical systems, or demonstrating business impact.

Red team engagements are ideal for organizations with mature security programs that want to test their detection and response capabilities under realistic conditions.

What We Test

  • Perimeter defenses and initial access vectors
  • Social engineering and human exploitation
  • Endpoint detection and response (EDR) evasion
  • Lateral movement and privilege escalation
  • Data access and exfiltration capabilities
  • Security Operations Center (SOC) detection and response effectiveness
  • Incident response procedures and escalation processes
  • Physical security controls (where in scope)

Deliverables

  • Full attack narrative documenting the engagement timeline
  • Detailed attack path diagrams and kill chain analysis
  • Assessment of detection and response capabilities
  • Purple team recommendations for improving defensive controls
  • Strategic roadmap for security program maturity

Cloud Security Testing

As organizations migrate critical workloads to the cloud, new attack surfaces emerge that require specialized security expertise. Our cloud security testing services assess your Amazon Web Services (AWS), Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud Platform (GCP) environments for misconfigurations, excessive permissions, insecure architectures, and exploitable vulnerabilities.

We evaluate your cloud infrastructure against industry benchmarks including CIS Benchmarks and cloud provider security best practices, while also performing hands-on exploitation to demonstrate real-world risk.

What We Test

  • Identity and Access Management (IAM) policies and roles
  • Storage bucket and blob permissions (S3, Azure Blob, GCS)
  • Network security groups and firewall rules
  • Serverless function security (Lambda, Azure Functions, Cloud Functions)
  • Container and Kubernetes security (EKS, AKS, GKE)
  • Secrets management and key rotation
  • Logging, monitoring, and alerting configurations
  • Cross-account and cross-tenant access risks
  • Privilege escalation paths within cloud environments

Deliverables

  • Cloud-specific vulnerability and misconfiguration report
  • Risk-rated findings with exploitation evidence
  • Architecture review and security recommendations
  • Compliance mapping (CIS, SOC 2, ISO 27001 where applicable)
  • Remediation guidance with cloud-native solutions

Wireless Network Penetration Testing

Wireless networks can provide attackers with a stealthy entry point into your corporate environment. Our wireless penetration testing services evaluate the security of your Wi-Fi infrastructure, identifying vulnerabilities in encryption, authentication, network segmentation, and access controls.

We assess your wireless environment for rogue access points, weak encryption protocols, misconfigured networks, and opportunities for unauthorized access. Our testers operate on-site to evaluate signal leakage, test for evil twin attacks, and attempt to pivot from the wireless network into your internal corporate infrastructure.

What We Test

  • Wireless encryption standards (WPA2/WPA3 Enterprise and Personal)
  • 802.1X authentication and RADIUS configurations
  • Rogue access point detection
  • Evil twin and man-in-the-middle attack scenarios
  • Wireless network segmentation (guest vs. corporate)
  • Signal leakage beyond physical boundaries
  • Bluetooth and other RF protocol security (where applicable)
  • Client isolation and network access controls
  • Captive portal security

Deliverables

  • Wireless security assessment report
  • Heat map of signal coverage and leakage areas
  • Identified rogue devices and unauthorized access points
  • Risk-rated findings with exploitation evidence
  • Remediation recommendations for wireless infrastructure hardening

Our Methodology

Every DragonShield engagement is grounded in industry-recognized methodologies and frameworks. We tailor our approach to each client's unique environment while maintaining the rigor and consistency that these standards demand.

PTES

Our primary engagement framework. PTES provides a structured seven-phase approach: pre-engagement interactions, intelligence gathering, threat modeling, vulnerability analysis, exploitation, post-exploitation, and reporting. This ensures comprehensive coverage and consistent quality across all engagements.

OWASP Testing Guide

The gold standard for web application security testing. We follow the OWASP Web Security Testing Guide (WSTG) for all web application assessments, covering 12 testing categories including information gathering, authentication testing, authorization testing, input validation, business logic testing, and API security.

NIST SP 800-115

The National Institute of Standards and Technology's Technical Guide to Information Security Testing and Assessment. We incorporate NIST guidance for planning, executing, and reporting on security assessments, ensuring our work aligns with federal standards and best practices.

OSSTMM

A comprehensive methodology for testing operational security across multiple domains. OSSTMM guides our approach to physical security, wireless security, human security, and telecommunications testing — ensuring we assess security holistically, not just technically.

How We Work

Our proven six-step engagement process ensures clarity, thoroughness, and actionable outcomes from start to finish.

1

Scoping & Planning

We begin with a detailed scoping call to understand your environment, objectives, compliance requirements, and concerns. Together, we define the engagement scope, rules of engagement, testing windows, and communication protocols. A formal Statement of Work and authorization documents are prepared and signed.

2

Reconnaissance & Intelligence Gathering

Our team conducts thorough reconnaissance of your target environment using both passive and active techniques. We gather open-source intelligence (OSINT), map your attack surface, identify technologies in use, and build a comprehensive picture of potential entry points.

3

Vulnerability Discovery & Analysis

Using a combination of automated scanning tools and manual testing techniques, we systematically identify vulnerabilities across the engagement scope. Each finding is validated manually to eliminate false positives and assess true exploitability.

4

Exploitation & Validation

With your authorization, we attempt to exploit identified vulnerabilities to demonstrate real-world impact. We document each successful exploitation with evidence, assess the potential business impact, and determine how far an attacker could progress from each entry point.

5

Reporting & Remediation Guidance

We compile our findings into a comprehensive report that includes an executive summary, detailed technical findings with CVSS scores, proof-of-concept evidence, and prioritized remediation recommendations. We present findings to both technical and executive stakeholders.

6

Remediation Support & Retesting

Our engagement doesn't end with the report. We offer remediation consultation to help your team address findings effectively, and we conduct retesting to verify that vulnerabilities have been successfully resolved.
